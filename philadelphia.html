About Philadelphia

Philadelphia is the largest city in Pennsylvania and is located in southeastern Pennsylvania at the confluence of the Delaware and Schuylkill rivers. It is one of the most historically important cities in the United States and remains a major cultural and economic center. [8]

More Facts About Philadelphia

  1. The grid of streets in central Philadelphia follows William Penn’s original plan and is described as the first U.S. city to be arranged that way. [8]
  2. Elfreth’s Alley, dating from 1702, is known as the oldest continuously inhabited street in the country. [8]
  3. The Liberty Bell was not rung on July 4, 1776; it was rung on July 8 for the first public reading of the Declaration of Independence. [9]
