About Harrisburg

Harrisburg is the capital of Pennsylvania and the seat of Dauphin County. Located on the east bank of the Susquehanna River, it serves as an important center for government and transportation in the region. [2]

Harrisburg City Information
City Attributes Information Source
Population Harrisburg had an estimated population of 50,649 in 2024. [1]
Year Incorporated Harrisburg was incorporated as a city in 1860. [2]
Region in Pennsylvania Harrisburg is located in South Central Pennsylvania. [3]
Classification Harrisburg is classified as an urban city. [3]
Average Income Compared to State Harrisburg’s median household income was $48,099, which was lower than Pennsylvania’s median household income of $77,971. [1], [4]

More Facts About Harrisburg

  1. Harrisburg began as “Harris’ Ferry,” after John Harris established a trading post and ferry service there in the early 1700s. [2]
  2. The current Pennsylvania State Capitol was completed in 1906 after the earlier capitol building was destroyed by fire in 1897. [5]
  3. President Theodore Roosevelt called the Pennsylvania State Capitol “the handsomest building I ever saw” at its dedication. [5]
